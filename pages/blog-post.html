The Perfect Truffle Risotto

October 15, 2025 | Chef Alessandro Marchetti | 5 min read

Risotto is more than just a dish—it's a meditation, a labor of love that rewards patience and attention to detail. Today, I'm sharing the secrets behind Savoria's signature truffle risotto.

The Foundation: Quality Ingredients

The beauty of risotto lies in its simplicity, which means every ingredient must be exceptional. We use Carnaroli rice from northern Italy—slightly firmer than Arborio, it creates the perfect creamy texture while maintaining that essential al dente bite.

Key Ingredients:

  • Carnaroli rice (1.5 cups)
  • Fresh black truffle (30g, thinly shaved)
  • Homemade vegetable stock (1 liter, kept warm)
  • Dry white wine (½ cup)
  • Parmigiano-Reggiano, aged 24 months (100g, grated)
  • Unsalted butter (4 tablespoons)
  • Shallots (2, finely minced)
  • Truffle oil (for finishing)

The Technique: Patience is Key

The secret to perfect risotto is constant attention. You cannot walk away—the rice demands your presence, your stirring, your love.

  1. Toast the rice: In a heavy-bottomed pan, melt half the butter and sauté shallots until translucent. Add rice and toast for 2-3 minutes until edges become slightly translucent.
  2. Add wine: Deglaze with white wine, stirring constantly until absorbed.
  3. The ritual begins: Add warm stock one ladle at a time, stirring continuously. Wait for each addition to be absorbed before adding the next. This process takes about 18-20 minutes.
  4. Test for doneness: The rice should be creamy but retain a slight firmness when bitten.
  5. Mantecatura: Remove from heat. Add remaining butter, Parmigiano, and half the shaved truffle. Stir vigorously for 2 minutes—this creates that signature wave-like consistency Italians call "all'onda."

"Risotto is not just about cooking rice; it's about creating an emotion on a plate."

Chef Alessandro Marchetti

The Savoria Touch

What elevates our truffle risotto to signature status? We infuse the stock with truffle peelings during preparation, creating layers of truffle flavor throughout. The final drizzle of white truffle oil and generous shaving of fresh black truffle create an aroma that's simply intoxicating.

Pro Tip

Never add cold stock to risotto. The temperature shock stops the cooking process and prevents the rice from releasing its starches properly. Always keep your stock at a gentle simmer.

Wine Pairing Suggestion

We recommend pairing this dish with a crisp white wine like Gavi di Gavi or a light-bodied Burgundy. The wine's acidity cuts through the richness while complementing the earthy truffle notes.

Come experience this dish at Savoria, where we've perfected it over thousands of servings. Or try making it at home—just remember, the secret ingredient is always love and patience.
